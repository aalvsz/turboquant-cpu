This process primarily occurs in the chloroplasts of plant cells, where chlorophyll captures sunlight. Water molecules are split, releasing oxygen as a byproduct, while carbon dioxide is taken from the atmosphere. The energy from sunlight is used to convert these raw materials into glucose and other sugars, which the plant uses for growth and energy.
